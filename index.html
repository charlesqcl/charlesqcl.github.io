Works

A Glance at Featured Projects.

My portfolio spans across a spectrum of disciplines, encompassing spatial data analysis, software development, web mapping, as well as architectural and media design.

City Science & Engineering

Journal Paper - 1st Author

AI, City Form and Land Surface Temperature. (view)

We construct a generative adversarial network (GAN) to predict land surface temperature maps with the input of land cover & land use maps in New York City. Environmental designers can get quick, fine-grained prediction and visualization of the environmental impacts of their proposals.

Conference Paper - 1st Author

Urban Design and Travel Behaviors. (view)

Based on the "5Ds" urban design & TOD framework, We use principal component analysis (PCA) and K-means clustering to analyze the GPS-based travel data of the Twin Cities region. Four trip clusters were concluded to provide insights for agencies seeking to better understand mobility patterns from a TOD perspective.

Journal Paper (Under Review) - 1st Author

AI, Urban Crimes and Active Transportation. (view)

Based on a framework of active transportation (AT) integrating Google Street View Images (GSVIs) with other urban data, we use multivariate regression (MLR) and geographically weighted regression (GWR) and find bike parking facilities, public restrooms and sky exposure have significant impacts on urban crimes.

Ongoing Research - collaborator

A Climate Justice Digital Twin for the Bay Area. (view)

We are developing an open-sourced website/digital twin integrating multi-scale air quality data and CDR data to inform sustainable environmental planning. The tool can inform different stakeholders about different social groups' exposure to polluted air. It is a start of a series of digital twins focusing on multiple cities and climate change.

Ongoing Research - initiator

ChatGPT/NLP, Social Media and Urban Functions. (view)

We are using multi-sourced urban data (especially social media data), neural networks and natural language processing methods (e.g., ChatGPT) to help different stakeholders (planners, investors and other publics) understand urban functions and street activities in a deeper way.

Ongoing Research - initiator

Intertwined Networks: Social Media and Risk Resilience.

We are studying the possibility of integrating social media networks to environmental sensing networks to develop an alert system for natural risks such as floods. The system should be able to provide real-time updates & recommendations based on machine learning algorithms.
